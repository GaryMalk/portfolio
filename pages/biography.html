Education Biography

Career Path Prior to Entering MAT Program

I worked in the software development industry for 26 years prior to entering the MAT program at CWU. I started as a manual tester hired as a contractor to work at Microsoft to test localized software on the Pan-Euro version of Windows 98, due to my fluency in Romanian and ability to speak and read German. My fluency in Romanian came about because I did humanitarian aid work in Romania for six years.

I progressed into test lead and test manager roles, learning how to write code for test automation and test tools, and eventually transitioned into software developer in test (SDET) roles. My last software industry position was Principal SDET at Puget Sound Energy.

What Led Me to Pursue Teaching

Most of my training has been on-the-job. In 2018, I decided to go back to school and earn a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science. This was after 20 years in the industry in senior development roles. I was able to accelerate and graduate in 2020 from Western Governors University (WGU). A lot had changed in higher education since I had been in school 40 years prior. I was inspired by the competency-based learning at WGU. Classes are 100% online, at your own pace. I had a required weekly phone call with my program mentor to verify I was staying on track. Classes are pass/fail and passing is considered at least 3.0.

This is revolutionary. What matters in competency-based learning is what you learned by the end of the process, not how well you performed while you were learning. Failing an exam at a traditional school means taking the whole class over again, wasting weeks on the sections you already know. In competency-based learning, you only study the parts you didn’t do well on and take the test again. Self-paced is good both for the highly capable who can accelerate and those needing extra help. More to the point, each individual can accelerate through sections that come easily or are already known and slow down for sections that are more difficult. Competency-based learning is at once democratizing, equitable, and accommodating.

I became intrigued with how my own early educational career might have been different. As I studied more, I learned that colleges and high schools are adopting competency-based learning along with “flipped classrooms”, where the students watch recorded lectures at home and come to class to get help with assignments. The instructor role transforms into tutor and curator.

More and more, I thought about this being the next act in my life. I’ve had my career. I could now teach and call it retirement. I wanted to be part of what’s changing in education.

My Academic Background

I studied Physics and Psychology at the University of Washington after high school. I also had extensive coursework in Mathematics and Chemistry.

While working humanitarian aid overseas, I studied the Management of Voluntary and Non-Profit Organizations through distance learning with Open University in the U.K., completing one year for a Certificate of Management.

I am largely self-taught in building computers and computer programming, as mentioned above. I obtained the Microsoft Certified Systems Engineer (MCSE) and Microsoft Certified Systems Administrator (MCSA) for Windows 2000 and became proficient in QA and C# while working in the industry. I later obtained a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from WGU, as mentioned above.
