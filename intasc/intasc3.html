InTASC Standard #3
Learning Environments

The teacher works with others to create environments that support individual and collaborative learning, and that encourage positive social interaction, active engagement in learning, and self motivation.

This standard is about building an environment that is conducive to learning. Expectations are high. Students feel safe; students work together collaboratively and the interactions are respectful. Students with learning differences get the support they need.

The artifacts below are some of the ways in which I met this standard:

  • "Human Bingo" Icebreaker for the first day of Personal Finance class. There were two versions of the Bingo form, passed out randomly to the students. Students went around talking to each other to collect signatures on their form. This icebreaker idea was shamelessly stolen from our own cohort icebreaker last summer.
  • History of Computer Science discussion questions. Students counted off into four groups by powers of 2 (2, 4, 8, 16 - hey, it's computer science class). Groups met after a PowerPoint presentation to discuss their answers. An example of things overheard from the groups: "I didn't know that almost all programmers used to be women, and now we're in the minority." Artifacts are three example student submissions.
  • Personal Finance class: Paying for College Unit: lesson on FAFSA: "ANALYZE: A FAFSA Submission Summary" assignment made available in English and Spanish. A sizeable number of the students in that class were Hispanic.
  • Hunt the Wumpus Group Assignments spreadsheet. Students worked together to create their group's version of the "Hunt the Wumpus" game as part of a competition sponsored by Microsoft. Microsoft volunteer advisors came two days a week to explain the requirements and assist the students.
