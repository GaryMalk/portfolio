InTASC Standard #5
Content Application

The candidate understands how to connect concepts and use differing perspectives to engage learners in critical thinking, creativity, and collaborative problem solving related to authentic local and global issues.

To satisfy this standard, students must be able to synthesize and apply the knowledge they've learned to solve problems relevent to themselves.

The students in my HTML classes are required to design and build a website that incorporates all the elements and techniques they've learned throughout the quarter. They decide the theme, come up with the content, decide on the fonts, color palette, and layouts, apply styling, and debug. They must follow accessibility guidelines and each page must pass validation. The websites are graded on completeness, appearance, and lack of errors. The students also critique each others's websites. I present their work as artifacts.

In my Personal Finance class, one of the most salient units was Buying a Car. High school students had no trouble finding this relevant. The artifact I present is a submission from one of the students for the lesson "Research: Find Your Next Car".
