InTASC Standard #2
Learning Differences

The teacher uses understanding of individual differences and diverse cultures and communities to ensure inclusive learning environments that enable each learner to meet high standards.

Students have different ways of learning and different learning goals. By giving students a choice of assignments to meet the learning objective and choice in how they complete it, the learning objective is contextualized to meet their needs and interests.

The School I Chose

Due: Friday, April 4, 2025 at 11:59 pm

This could be any school, college, university, or trade school.

In the unit  Deep Dive: Deciding Where to Go to School you may have completed this choice activity: Deep Dive: School of Interest

Start with that information and write a report on the school you finally picked (or your current top contender). If you didn't do that choice activity, you can do it now because you will need that information in your report:

  • Name of the school
  • Tuition, fees, expenses
  • Net price calculation
  • What you plan to do for housing and meals
  • Percentage admission rate
  • Percentage graduation rate
  • Your degree program
  • National or Regional Ranking
  • Regional Accreditation
  • Program Accreditation

Add to that an essay on:

  • What attracted you to the school
  • At least three positive things about the school that influenced your decision
  • At least one negative thing you also considered
  • Your experience at a campus visit, if you had one. If not, are you planning to visit? When?
  • Did you meet with any current students? What questions did you ask? If not, what questions would you ask?
  • What clubs, sports, organizations, or other extra-curricular activities do you plan to do? If you met with any of the coaches, members, or staff during your visit, talk about what you got out of it.
  • Did you attend a sports event or other activity during a campus visit?
  • PHOTOS! Pictures of campus, pictures of you on campus, pictures of you in the school sweatshirt standing in front of a big sign with the name, or anything like that to give us a feel for what it is like at the school.

You can do this all in a Word or Google doc, but I would love to see PowerPoint presentations.

We will have time to do this in class this week. It is not due until Friday.

Campus Visits

 Due: Friday, April 4, 2025 at 11:59 pm

 Write a report on your experience visiting college, university, or trade school campuses.

Your report should include this info:

  • What schools did you visit?
  • Where is each one located?
  • What were your positive and negative impressions after each visit?
  • What did you learn about your degree program that you didn't already know? How did that impact your decision?
  • Did you meet current students and what questions did they answer for you?
  • What extra-curricular activities at each school interest you?
  • Did you attend any sports events or other events?
  • Describe the surrounding area of each school and how that influences your decision.
  • What sites did you see while you were there?

You can do this all in a Word or Google doc, but I would love to see PowerPoint presentations.

We will have time to do this in class this week. It is not due until Friday.
