InTASC Standard #10
Leadership and Collaboration

The candidate seeks appropriate leadership roles and opportunities to take responsibility for student learning, to collaborate with learners, families, colleagues, other school professionals, and community members to ensure learner growth, and to advance the profession.

There are many forms of leadership. Leadership is thinking beyond yourself to what is good for the your school and community. Having a vision for where you want to go, planning how to get there, and inspiring others to join you is clearly leadership, and we think of such people as charasmatic. But there are other types of leaders, and here's the key: leadership can be distributed. Some provide vital expertise and services. Some act in an advisory capacity, stepping back giving perspective. Even just showing up faithfully is leadership, because without a cadre of reliable, supportive volunteers every organization would fail.

Hunt the Wumpus

Spring quarter the students in my C# classes were assigned into development teams to create a computer game for the “Hunt the Wumpus” competition sponsored by Microsoft. The students were assigned areas of code to create, such as cave system, score board, player, and movement. They had to learn to work together to make it happen, with me, my mentor, and the Microsoft volunteers who came twice a week acting as advisors.

I taught the students about source control, how to create a code repository on GitHub, and how to merge code so that multiple developers can work together in the same repository. I gave advice, montiored progress, and helped to debug and sometimes detangle failed merges. The students were required to add me as a collaborator on their repositories. Artifacts presented are the assignment in Schoology requiring students to do this, a screenshot of an email inviting me to collaborate, and a screenshot of a GitHub repository.

On the big day we took the two classes to Microsoft for the judging competition with several other schools competing. Three of our eight teams brought home trophies, one of which is also presented as an artifact.

Cross-Discipline Instruction

As a college student, I always enjoyed the way history was interwoven into my science and mathematics classes. I teach my computer science classes that way, too, because it's important for students to understand their place in history, where it started, and where it's going. Presented as an artifact is the PowerPoint of The History of Computer Science, which is my favorite lecture to deliver.

This last year I also taught Personal Finance, including a unit on Paying for College. One of the lessons I developed was on winning scholarships, with an assigned scholarship essay. Teaching how to write an essay was language arts. Students were taught that the elements of a winning scholarhip essay are:

  • Core Values
  • Insights
  • Vulnerability
  • Craft
  • A tie-in to your educational and career goals
  • How you will give back

Toastmasters Club

I've been a Toastmaster for five years. I've been an officer in multiple roles, including club president and area director. As an area director, I visited the clubs assigned to me and worked with them to acheive success. We were awarded President's Distinguished Area, the only area in the district to acheive that status. My artifact is a screenshot of the email from our district director notifying me of this.

The computer science curriculum in most colleges focuses on technical skills, yet communication and leadership skills are critical in every career. Toastmasters can bridge that gap. While one must be over 18 to be a member of Toastmasters, high school students can participate in the Youth Leadership Program. It is my desire to sponsor one at my school.

PTA Membership

As a dad, I've been a member of the PTA, regularly attending meetings and volunteering. I've been a field trip chaperone, volunteered in a classroom for a day, and helped with field day. Because of my past advocacy for foster kids, I had experience working with the legislature so I was Legislative Liason two years in a row and organized kids to come to Olympia on PTA Lobby Day (usually on MLK Day). I plan to suport the PTA as a teacher as well. My artifact is a photo of my D.O.G.S. (Dads of Great Students) t-shirt, worn while volunteering.

Community Leadership

I'm a member of my church's charity committee, and have organized or assisted in multiple programs, including food drives for the Woodinville Storehouse food bank and toiletries drives for the homeless women and their families who live in "Safe Parking" at a local church.
