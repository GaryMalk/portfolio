InTASC Standard #9
Professional Learning and Ethical Practice

The candidate engages in ongoing professional learning and uses evidence to continually evaluate his/her practice, particularly the effects of his/her choices and actions on others (learners, families, other professionals, and the community), and adapts practice to meet the needs of each learner.

The essence of this standard is continuous improvement: on-going self-reflection, seeking out feedback and constructive feedback, being dedicated to on-going development of my skills as a teacher and in my content areas of computer science and mathematics, and keeping abreast of new developments. I also want to be sensitive in my interactions in our community of learners, both with students and teachers, and to adapt my methods to meet the needs of others.

My artifacts for this are:

  • My Professional Growth Plan, which I shared with my host-school mentor and with my field supervisor. Areas identified for growth were aligned with Learner Development. I am particularly interested in ELL students and how I can better meet their needs. In computer science and mathematics are international; there are many learning resources that can help on the internet. Mathematics and computer programming are both in a real sense languages, so the ELL has skills to draw on to do that. I want to learn better how to assist that process. I also want to continually keep my own technical skills up to date.
  • National IT Innovation Center (NITIC) Summer 2024 Working Connections: Cyber Buffet Certificate of Completion. This was a week-long seminar for college computer science instructors. The track I attended was Cyber Buffet, where we learned how to detect and combat computer viruses. The class provided tools to use for developing our own classes to teach it.
